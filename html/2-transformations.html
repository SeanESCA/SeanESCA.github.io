2 Transformations

2.1 Applying Transformations to Functions

There are three possible types of transformations you can be asked to apply to a function: translations, reflections, and stretches. Given the curve y=f(x)y = f(x),

  • a translation by the vector (ab)\begin{pmatrix}a \\ b\end{pmatrix} gives the curve y=f(x−a)+by = f(x - a) + b.

  • a reflection in the x-axis gives y=−f(x)y = -f(x).

  • a reflection in the y-axis gives y=f(−x)y = f(-x).

  • a stretch by a scale factor of cc in the direction of y-axis gives y=a⋅f(x)y = a \cdot f(x).

  • a stretch by a scale factor of cc in the direction of x-axis gives y=f(xa)y = f \left(\frac{x}{a}\right).

You may find it helpful to visualise the curve y=f(x)y = f(x) before and after each transformation, for which Desmos’s graphing calculator can be helpful.
