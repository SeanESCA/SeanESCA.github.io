1 Powers and Roots

1.1 Surds

A surd is an expression of the form a+ba + \sqrt{b}, where aa and b≥0b \ge 0 are rational numbers.

1.1.1 Rationalising the Denominator

Suppose that we have a fraction ya+b\frac{y}{a + \sqrt{b}}, where the denominator contains a surd. Rationalising the denominator essentially means removing the surd from the denominator. We can achieve this by multiplying the numerator and denominator by a−ba - \sqrt{b}:

ya+b=y(a−b)(a+b)(a−b)=y(a−b)a2−b.\begin{align*} \frac{y}{a + \sqrt{b}} &= \frac{y(a - \sqrt{b})}{(a + \sqrt{b})(a - \sqrt{b})} \\ &= \frac{y(a - \sqrt{b})}{a^{2} - b}. \end{align*}

Since aa and bb are rational (from the definition of a surd), so will the denominator a2−ba^{2} - b.
